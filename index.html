Take A Glimpse Inside The Book

4 Important Concepts You’ll Master

  1. Did you know that the rate at which the world is changing is accelerating? Today, the world moves about 6.5x faster than it did a hundred years ago, and it's only going to get worse. With each year, the world changes faster, and the information in this book becomes more valuable.
  2. Ever have trouble finding the information you're looking for? This book will teach you how to find it and how to go about a serious research campaign, but, more importantly, it will teach you when it's time to move on. Some things aren't worth reading, even if you're half way through them.
  3. Supercharge your understanding. Ever feel frustrated trying to understand a new piece of technology or concept? Minimize those moments. The lessons in the second chapter will push you towards eureka.
  4. As far as we can tell, the human mind has no limits when it comes to long-term memory -- so why do we so often forget something we looked up yesterday? (Can you remember what you had to eat last week?) This book will teach the most important laws of human memory. Cut your rate of forgetting in half (or better!).

About The Author

Hey, I'm Robert Seaton, and that's my face to the left of this sentence (although, to be 100% honest, I suspect that picture makes my neck look too long. Like a giraffe with a killer smile.)

I write the blog you came here from, and I wrote this entire page, and now I've written a book. About dealing with the complexities of modern life. Maybe you're wondering: Why did I write it? Well, like a lot of things, it was born out of frustration. What passes for education today is so far out of touch with the actual demands placed on you and me that, well, it makes me want to pull out all of my hair, starting from the getting-sorta-long hairs on my head down to the small ones populating my big toe.

At least, that's why I started writing. I kept writing because I'm passionate about helping you out. I want you to achieve to your goals, whatever they are, and I want to do everything I can to help you with those goals. Seriously! That's why this book is free -- that's why I write. Not to discover myself, or to spread some half-baked theory, but to help out you. To make the world less daunting, to make life more like living and less like a mad rush towards... what, exactly?
